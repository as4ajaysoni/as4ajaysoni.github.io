Why Ventilation Matters

Indoor Air Can Be 5-10x More Polluted
🌬️

The Hidden Danger Indoors

In sealed modern buildings, indoor air can be 5-10x more polluted than outdoor air. Poor ventilation leads to respiratory issues, reduced cognitive function, and increased disease spread. In crisis situations, clean air becomes a survival priority.

Open windows daily for cross-ventilation. Install exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms. Aim for 6-8 air changes per hour.
🏠

Natural Ventilation Methods

Cross-ventilation uses opposing windows to create airflow. Stack ventilation uses temperature differences to pull air up. Passive cooling uses thermal mass and shading. These methods are energy-free and highly effective.

Position windows on opposite walls. Use window vents and ceiling fans. Plant trees for natural shading.
🔧

Mechanical Ventilation Systems

Exhaust fans remove stale air from kitchens, bathrooms, and utility areas. Supply fans bring fresh air in. Balanced systems do both. Heat recovery ventilators (HRVs) exchange air while retaining heat.

Bathroom fans: 50-150 CFM. Kitchen range hoods: 300+ CFM. Run fans 20 min after cooking/showering.
📊

Air Quality Monitoring

Measure CO2 levels (under 1000 ppm is good), humidity (40-60% ideal), and temperature. Smart monitors alert you to problems. Poor air quality causes fatigue, headaches, and long-term health issues.

CO2 monitors cost ₹2,000-₹5,000. Place in bedrooms and living areas. Monitor especially while sleeping.

HVAC Systems Explained

Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning
❄️

Air Conditioning Types

Split ACs cool individual rooms. Central ACs cool entire homes. Window units are cheaper but noisier. Inverter ACs save energy by varying compressor speed. Choose based on room size and cooling needs.

1 ton AC cools approx 100-150 sq ft. Always buy inverter models. Check star ratings for energy efficiency.
🔥

Heating Solutions

Inverter ACs can heat in winter. Room heaters (oil-filled, fan, radiant) are portable options. Under-floor heating is luxurious but expensive. Heat pumps provide efficient heating and cooling in one system.

Oil-filled radiators are safest. Gas heaters need ventilation. Heat pumps have highest efficiency (300-400%).
🔄

Ducting & Distribution

Central AC uses ductwork to distribute air. Flexible ducts are easier to install but lose efficiency. Rigid ducts perform better. Annual professional cleaning prevents mold and maintains airflow.

Clean or replace ducts every 3-5 years. Check filters monthly. Sealed ducts prevent energy loss.
⚡

Energy Efficiency Tips

Smart thermostats learn your schedule and optimize temperature. Proper insulation reduces load. Regular maintenance keeps efficiency high. Inverter technology can reduce electricity bills by 30-50%.

Set thermostat to 24-26°C. Use fan mode when comfortable. Clean filters monthly. Annual professional servicing.

Air Purification

Removing Pollutants & Pathogens
🔄

HEPA Filters Explained

HEPA (High-Efficiency Particulate Air) filters capture 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger. This includes dust, pollen, mold spores, bacteria, and some viruses. True HEPA certification is essential.

HEPA filters capture 99.97% of particles. Replace every 6-12 months. Look for H13 or H14 grade.
🌿

Activated Carbon Filters

Activated carbon removes gases, odors, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that HEPA can't. Combined with HEPA, you get complete air cleaning. Carbon filters need replacement every 3-6 months.

Essential for kitchens, smoking areas, near paints/chemicals. Replace carbon filters every 3-6 months.
⚡

UV-C Light Sanitization

UV-C light kills bacteria, viruses, and mold spores in the air passing through. It's installed in HVAC ducts or standalone units. UV-C is highly effective but requires proper installation for safety.

UV-C bulbs last 1-2 years. Requires professional installation. Don't expose skin or eyes to UV-C light.
📏

Choosing the Right Purifier

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) measures performance. Calculate: CADR should be at least 2/3 of room size. For 300 sq ft room, need CADR of 200+. Consider noise levels, filter cost, and energy use.

Calculate: CADR at least 2/3 room size. For 300 sq ft, need CADR 200+. Place in bedroom for best results.

Humidity & Temperature Control

The Sweet Spot for Health
💧

Why Humidity Matters

Mold, bacteria, and viruses thrive in humidity above 60%. Maintaining 40-60% relative humidity inhibits pathogens. Too low (below 30%) causes dry skin, irritated eyes, and respiratory issues.

Use hygrometers to monitor. Keep between 40-60% for optimal health and mold prevention.
🌫️

Dehumidification

Dehumidifiers remove excess moisture from air. They're essential in humid climates, basements, and during monsoon. Refrigerant dehumidifiers are common; desiccant types work in lower temperatures.

Target 40-50% humidity. Run in bathrooms after showers, basements, and storage areas. Clean filters regularly.
🚿

Humidification

Central humidifiers integrate with HVAC. Room humidifiers are portable. Ultrasonic types are quiet and efficient. Proper humidity prevents dry skin, wood cracking, and reduces static electricity.

Use cool-mist for safety around children. Clean daily to prevent mold. Maintain 40%+ humidity in winter.
🌡️

Optimal Temperature Settings

WHO recommends 18-24°C for indoor spaces. Higher temperatures increase energy use; too low affects comfort. Sleep better at 18-20°C. Adjust for activity level and clothing.

24-26°C is energy-efficient. 18-20°C for sleeping. Layer clothing rather than raising temperature.
